Fuzhou => Singapore => Kyiv => Istanbul => Helsinki

Lu / 璐 (Pinyin: Lù; “fine jade”)

I'm Lu, a designer and artist currently based in Helsinki. Before moving to Finland, I studied information systems and have worked as an interaction and UX designer in Singapore. In a side project with friends, we prototyped an urban farming platform to promote cross-generational communication. This prototype won awards in a design jam themed "design for a thriving community" by Adobe. After this, I became interested in the less-visible parts of design, the systems and their interactions beyond the digital interface. With the interest in communities and services, I started pursuing my study in design and media at Aalto University.

Like the life in the tropics and the Nordics, I'm intrigued by the connections between seemingly contrasting things. Through (design) practices, I explore the surrounding world, study a certain phenomenon, communicate the findings, and thus connect the loose parts together. For me, design is the reflection-in-action, a bridge between vita activa (active life) and vita contemplativa (comtemplative life).

I'm looking for jobs in service design and design research. If you would like to get in touch, feel free to contact me through email chenlu.ciel@gmail.com or my LinkedIn Lu Chen (Design & Research)

on focus

In the autumn of 2021, I was working on a school project about designing advice services for Finnish expatriates (as the design brief described). The word “expatriate” intrigued me a lot: who are expatriates? How are they different from immigrants? Am I an expatriate - and why do I never feel this way?
